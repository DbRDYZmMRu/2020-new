         Of all that I’ve read so far in the introductory chapter - the beginning - of the book “a brief history of mathematical thought” this has me rapt:  mathematical truths are of a piece with the only world we can know, even if they are further abstracted from experience than most of the truths we are interested in… physical reality is not the same as our conception of it. From our perceptual inference there can be no infinitely precise truth, conversely there can be unambiguous deductions. 

 

         A beautiful quote from the same book, it induces a deep sense of identity, an affirmation of the intrinsic nature of the symmetrical mirror of nature and consciousness in a way that still, delineates  ‘In arithmetic we are not concerned with objects which we come to know as something alien from without through the medium of the senses, but with objects given directly to our reason and, as its nearest kin, utterly transparent to it.’ — Gottlob Frege, 1848–1925 

 

 

 

 

Night Visions:

 

         I had a night vision of my dad trying to dispose the palm of the coconut tree, but instead of having it tossed over the gate, as is his custom, in front of the house, he topples it over the fence, into the next compound. While trying to do this with its stalk, its rear end, just left in view, still in the compound, it hits him in the head and he falls lifeless on the ground - unconscious - strange right? Well I ran up to him, shouting out almost, I felt less emotion, well, as a viewer.

 

 

         I think I may just be at a crossroad, seeing the outcome of trying to hurt someone else, when it isn’t necessary. Moving forward, regardless of whatever is thrown at me, I will remain in my secret place; unperturbed, not going down the same lane of the disturber.
