         Back at my time at Wiayu, the ingenuous me, easily trusting, quick to embrace everyone’s motive as of good intention. How do I move forward from here? How do I tread the line between being a cynic and being receptive?

 

         In today’s musical, I discovered Joseph Haydn, a great composer of the 18th century, I’ll be getting his works, it’s said—there is a piece of string quartet for every mood.

 

         The habit of brushing “scruffy” nose, a carefree attitude towards immediate urgency, that’s what it is. The need for having a proper way of heuristic learning, memory aiding, mnemonics maybe? I need to figure other methods of remembering. Putting markers on things as store. When I can’t control these agents, it won’t work in remembering, maybe comprehending not remembering.

 

         My heart is still broken as it were, and this feeling I have for Jessica is going nowhere.

 

         A quote from the book Messiah quoting Jung:  When we analyse the persona we strip off the mask, and discover that what seemed to be individual is at bottom collective; in other words, that the persona was only a mask of the collective psyche. Fundamentally the persona is nothing real: it is a compromise between individual and society as to what a man should appear to be . . . The persona is a semblance, a two-dimensional reality, to give it a nickname. 

 

         A psychological instrument in the Stoic tool kit is negative visualization. It’s imperative involves making a point of imagining ways in which my life could be worse. It is important to realize that in advising me to negatively visualize, the Stoics aren’t advocating that I dwell on how things could be worse; that would indeed be a recipe for misery. Instead, what I should do is periodically have flickering thoughts about how my life and circumstance could be worse.
