Devotional: PAY YOUR TAXES

 

Poem of the Day: Ode to the Loss of the Titanic by Geoffrey Hill

 

 

 

 

Entries:

 

         Today in history, early on the morning of 1912, RMS Titanic sank in the icy waters of the North Atlantic, over 1500 people died, almost 3/4 of the passengers and crew on board at the time, it is fixed in the popular imagination as one of the shocking maritime disasters of all time.


         A life oriented  to leisure  is in the end a life  oriented to death —the  greatest leisure of all .

 

         The aim is to be political, intellectual, and artistic.

 

         I wake to sleep, and take my waking slow. I feel my fate in what I cannot fear. I learn by going where I have to go.

 

—Theodore Roethke, The Waking
