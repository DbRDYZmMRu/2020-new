Devotional: WHAT KIND OF BOXER ARE YOU

 

Poem of the Day: Friends by Polly Clark

 

 

 

 

Entries:

 

         Halo, I didn’t get to read the psycho-epistemological discourse on art yesterday, there was no electricity, I will today though.

 

         I learned today the cause of trends, it’s philosophy . . . from the Romantic Manifesto of art by Ayn Rand.

 

         An excerpt from a reading of the Romantic Manifesto by Ayn Rand:  The course of mankind’s   progress   is not a straight, automatic line, but a tortuous struggle, with long detours or relapses into the stagnant night of the irrational. Mankind moves forward by the grace of those   human bridges   who are able to   grasp and transmit  , across years or centuries, the achievements men had reached—and to carry them further.  A representation of what I hope my life represents, leaving my job to understand my roots, better building on my persona, understanding what ticks, and curbing ticks that deter motion in progress.

 

         I think it’s safe to say; draw the consensus, that the phenomenon discussed earlier in this journal is finally understood, the constant insurgency and disturbance well known, is a direct result of the detour of the human into a subterranean sensory level of awareness, a state that is sub-perceptual—the decline into the sub-animal, the acculturation to the barbaric.

 

         A fact to keep with me as I progress, is the fact that art, like literature is not a social endeavour, a reminder to revoke the impose in surfeit of the  darkseid .

 

         The constant suggestions and tries at “elucidating”   or analysing art—music, movies, etc. — is a contrived attempt at impeding the growth of the individual, the insularly altruistic nature of the avatar by agents with sub-animal agendas. This voids the individual of the much needed expression of the mind through art. A crime of the spirit.
