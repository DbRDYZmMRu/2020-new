Devotional: TO EACH HIS OWN

 

Poem of the Day: The Poetry Grand National by Roger Stevens

 

 

 

 

Entries:

 

         Today is another amazing day, I wake up a bit offset from my conditioned norm, but It’s fine, I wake up, get my thoughts together, strobe my brain to action, and get to the day’s waking motions. It’s a bit of a drill, getting these thoughts down; filling up my journal. Always an urge to get the devotional opened early in the day, but I find myself still sleepy as I said earlier, it’s more of an environment thing than a personal thing, it’s fine though, head is above water, and I’m striding on with my sight on both ends of my peripheral vision clearly perceived.

My dad’s out, I’m filling up his deposit of water, I slide to his sitting room, find his bag a bit out of place, I peer in, find an A4 sheet, turn it over, discovering a list of some sort for an association on commercial motorcycle taxis for a particular geographical area, only to find my name as vice-chairman. Okay that’s a rude way of discovering anything that consequential, I don’t know what to think of it, I’m just a bit aloof about it. Choosing not to give it much thought, I’ll just let it slide. It’s fine.

 

         An excerpt from today’s reading of city girls by Elizabeth Gilbert:  He preferred the grit and glitter of the theatre world to the polish and repression of café society .

 

         We used to be interested in inquisitive studying and learning, now all we care for is to indulge in mindless discussions amid gallivanting acts.
