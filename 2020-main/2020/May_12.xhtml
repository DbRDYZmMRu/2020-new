Devotional: KINDNESS IS ALWAYS THE RIGHT RESPONSE

 

Poem of the Day: On a Lane in Spring by John Clare

 

 

 

 

Entries:

 

         First, I must say, this is the first time I’m having a serendipity of this nature. But It’s safe to say, in the final analysis of things, there is a beautiful seam woven, drawn through all things; not as a delineator but as a hitch. I’m not one to root for the concepts of synchronicity as an objective way of analysing perceptual inference—for mental health reasons. But an allusion to its nature can be a fond conclusion for the uninitiated—the untrained mind. The psychoanalytic methodology of dream interpretation is sacrosanct in understanding events, or more broadly: the articulated sphere of being. I skimmed a book in the arts, and to that, it suggested dreams mean nothing; madness you will find there. In point, the event of this morning—my inability to restrain myself—leading to a little outburst, a result of my emotional frustration at the constant discord reactions; a misjudgement of the constant frivolities, including chores. My opinionated ideas on the importance of a family’s need for an ideal that supports a conduct that reflects success is paramount. It’s important the individual doesn’t lose sense of his identity. A dream says a lot and I had one—a reminder, to never walk naked.
