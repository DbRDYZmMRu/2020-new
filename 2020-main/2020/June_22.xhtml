         Understanding the difference between insanity and a pretence of value provoked out of deep-seated empirical based meaning is to delve into the abject misery of insanity. My informed narrative is born of apprehension—resolved in a feat. A trickle of constant misinformation holds no water at my basal contingent. The future stands in objective discernment for those with the right mantle. Words elude those without understanding, a loss of the fact that  truth is truth , never wavering.  For the physical science is a substrate on which the humanities should stand , any view away from this is a fallacy, a cognitive bias, a deep-running crippling daemon process; a final hindsight bias.

 

         Language is ever evolving, changing to meet the growing needs of an ever developing world. The Yoruba language in its colloquial use, remains one of a myriad of impossible feats; a range of symbols forcing itself on an ever growing expanding meaning set. Grouping in bands, a growing nuanced substrate. The result is a blindness so great. Even a borrowing of a foreign language can’t fix. In my inmate state of mind; current conditioning, I say and state clearly out of remiss: I will never ever keep to heart the words of a tribe that chases insanity in an ever twisting monotony of already based meaning, the overuse of trite words in describing largely disparate domains. To not change or grow in that regard is to chase flies on a sore hollowed.

What permits continuity in the path of understanding is the honest endeavour, even if it means, reading some paragraphs again.

         I think I am Nightswan now, I was Theonator, then I was Theophil.  I am born anew now . A commentary from a text I wrote some time ago.

 

 My music, musings . . . the flower, the net profit of my existence . . . the living being 

 Theo from Theos, meaning God 

 Phil, an affinity for a thing, origin from the word Phile, meaning loving. 

 TheoPhil means an affinity for the creator, the supreme being, the love. God loving. 

 I got this idea on my first use of empathogens, weed to be precise, I had this feeling of being so loved, I felt all songs were renditions portraying the inherent love bursting in the emotions of the singers or artist; an allusion, the personalisation of the love. 

 Subliminally came this name on a day of writing, I was TheoNator before my first weed use. 

 I got the name Theonator from Terminator, more like God detonator or destroyer, I grew up mad at God, like a phobia, not liking him; having not answered my prayers. Couldn't deduce why my lost textbooks wouldn't materialize in my room. 

 A journey of Theonator to TheoPhil. Here I am, TheoPhiL the often inordinate . . . rapture singer.
