         I see my dad first, while seeing unconditionally. Beautiful.

 

         Today, the cuckoo day, the most famous sound alarm clocks make. Just by the knowledge of this, I learn every day from my daily poem listening and stoic devotionals. Like how today’s input is about actually knowing what matters in life and giving it attention.

 

         An excerpt from a book I skimmed through Creating Characters: How to Build Story People:  In essence, boredom, I discovered (though it’s hardly a tremendous or unique insight), is a conflict state, in which duty, conditioning, or some other element demands you do one thing, when consciously or otherwise, you want to be doing something else. 

 

         Excerpt from the fore-mentioned book, touching on the recent drop in output in my musical compositions:  The well, of course, is your own head. Your brain. Your consciousness. Your imagination. You’ve drained it of things that interest and intrigue you. Or, to put it another way, you’ve used the same story elements too often: the same ideas, the same settings, the same twists and complications, the same characters.
