Devotional: TEST YOUR IMPRESSIONS

 

Poem of the Day: The Ballad of Semmerwater by William Watson

 

 

 

 

Entries:

 

         Today is a gloomy day with soft showers sprinkling, it’s 9:09 AM, I hear a neighbour shouting in her native language:  you’ll get the beating . It’s one of the ever vague cries I hear when at home, it’s imposing but not holding. Today’s poem is about to be listened to, I wrote the verse two for  Beat me down , another great song in my colossus, I’m happy with my streak, it’s a clean one.

 

         One of the key functions of thinking is to reverse positions, a method I should insert in my thinking scheme.

 

         When using  and  to group items,  is  can be used to define the state. 

 

         A lightness of being, an awareness of play, of possibility.

 

 

 

 

Night Visions:

 

         Moral of today’s poem the ballad of Semmerwater goes thus: Even the proudest of empires will fall if it earns the curse of its poor and vulnerable.
